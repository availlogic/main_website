Global Humanitarian Agency Transforms Emergency Response with AI and a Modern Data Platform

At a Glance

Client

A premier United Nations humanitarian agency, operating in dozens of countries to assist tens of millions of people.

Challenge

Disparate, siloed data systems and an inability to process new data types hindered critical analysis and slowed emergency response.

Solution

An end-to-end, cloud-native data and AI platform, including a unified data warehouse, computer vision models, and a predictive analytics engine.

Outcome

Launched a dynamic, public-facing global monitoring platform, providing near-real-time insight to anticipate crises and optimise resource allocation.

Introduction

A leading global humanitarian organisation, operating under a United Nations mandate, is responsible for delivering life-saving assistance to tens of millions of people in over 80 countries each year. The effectiveness of these operations depends entirely on the ability to predict and respond to crises with speed and accuracy.

The Challenge: When Data Silos Cost Time and Obscure Insight

As the agency's operations grew, its existing data infrastructure became a serious bottleneck. Key data was trapped in disparate, isolated databases, making comprehensive analysis slow and manual. The agency was data-rich but information-poor, and its inability to integrate and analyse diverse data streams meant that responses were often reactive.

The agencyâ€™s leadership initiated a full-fledged digital transformation to apply modern cloud computing, big data, and AI to shorten emergency response times and optimise its global operations.

Our Solution: Engineering a Platform for Global Insight

We were selected as the technical engineering partner to build the foundational platform for this vision. Our solution was a comprehensive, multi-layered platform engineered to ingest, process, analyse, and visualise data from every corner of the agency's operations.

1. The Foundation: A Unified, Cloud-Native Data Warehouse

The first and most critical step was to break down the data silos. We engineered a modern data platform to serve as the organisation's single source of truth, building robust data integration pipelines and a two-tiered storage architecture (a data lakehouse and cloud object storage).

2. AI-Powered Insight: Computer Vision for On-the-Ground Awareness

We developed two bespoke computer vision solutions to provide objective, scalable insights from remote areas. One model analysed satellite imagery to detect and count huts in refugee camps, while a second set of models analysed drone and field imagery to estimate crowd sizes and demographics during aid distribution.

3. The "Crown Jewel": Predictive Analytics for Food Security

The ultimate goal was to predict, rather than just react to, food security crises. With the unified data warehouse, this became possible. We worked with the agency's experts to develop and deploy a suite of sophisticated forecasting models that analyse thousands of key metrics to forecast the future of food security and automatically generate alerts.

4. Visualisation: Delivering Actionable Insights to All Stakeholders

Finally, we ensured this wealth of new information was accessible and actionable. We integrated the platform with the agency's existing BI tools and built a powerful, interactive global map that provides a simple, visual overview of food security across the globe.

The Outcome: A New Global Standard in Data-Driven Humanitarian Aid

The solution culminated in the launch of the agency's public-facing, dynamic global hunger monitoring platform. This system has fundamentally transformed the organisation's ability to achieve its mandate.

The platform provides a single, trusted source of truth that empowers every level of the organisation. Most profoundly, the predictive analytics engine has shifted the agency's posture from reactive to proactive. They can now anticipate emerging crises, plan more effective interventions, and allocate resources with data-driven precision, ultimately shortening response times and saving more lives.
